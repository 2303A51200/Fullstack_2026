Key findings summary
Primary driver
CO₂ from fossil fuels and industrial processes accounts for 74% of all GHG emissions by CO₂e.
GWP ≠ impact
SF₆ has GWP of 23,500 but trace abundance. Volume × potency together determine real-world warming.
All gases rising
CO₂, CH₄, and N₂O all show nearly linear upward trends since 1980 — current controls are insufficient.

Climate change effects
Extreme heat waves
Frequency and intensity of heat events increasing with each 0.1°C rise.
Flooding & coastlines
Extreme rainfall, sea-level rise, and coastline erosion accelerating.
Wildfires
Augmented risk from drier conditions and higher ambient temperatures.
Ozone depletion
N₂O consumes stratospheric ozone; halocarbons further erode the layer.
Water cycle shifts
Warmer air holds more vapor — intensifying both drought and precipitation.
Soil & eutrophication
Changes in moisture, acidification, and nutrient runoff affect ecosystems.

Policy timeline — key milestones
1987
Montreal Protocol — first global treaty restricting halocarbons to protect the ozone layer.
1997
Kyoto Protocol — binding GHG reduction targets for developed nations.
2015
Paris Agreement — 196 nations commit to limit warming to 1.5–2°C above pre-industrial levels.
2022
All three major GHGs (CO₂, CH₄, N₂O) reach all-time high concentrations — current policies insufficient.
Now
Urgent scale-up of capture tech, renewable transition, and agricultural emission controls required.
